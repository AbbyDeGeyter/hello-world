Education

Education is an important part of my life I believe that we become well rounded individuals through the consumption of knowledge. My educational journey started out rough I was indecisive as to where I felt most pulled for a future career. I began in nursing, then to exploratory and began my sophomore year at IU as a comprehensive design major with a minor in HCI. Throughout my sophomore year I found myself enjoying my HCI courses more than my courses as SOAD which ultimately lead me to switch my major and minor. I find that my love for creativity and design gives me an interesting perspective on the world of technology.

A B.S. degree in informatics involves learning both technical, organizational and communication skills that help to facilitate beneficial intersections between technology and people. Learn more at IU's degree program page

A cognate in graphic design allows me to focus on using technology to create visual designs, from logos, to websites. Through the cognate I am able to develop skills in the principals of design such as typography, style, text and graphic placement, and production. I am able to then use these gained skills and tools to solve problems, improve an online experience and produce a variety of digital elements and visual communications.

Through a Human-Centered Computing minor I am expanding my knowledge to understanding how people develop and change technology, which in turn changes people. I am able to gain insight into other areas of study such as he role of design in the creation of new technology, multimedia technology, and Legal, ethical, and organizational implications of technology.
